For example, if your code is the rules of Rock Paper Scissors, your first abstract query might be "how can someone win a two player game", and the abstract answers are in the form " the first player throws rock, and the second player throws scissors, and the first player wins". That would be one of the three possible abstract models that answer the hypothetical query. Now I know William and Jason played a game, and I know William threw rock. If what I want to know is who won a game, I have two options. I can run the same query again against the rules, with the concrete facts I have, and ask it to presume only that Jason threw something. The complexity of the first query is exponential with regard to the complexity of the rules and the number of hypothetical inputs.
