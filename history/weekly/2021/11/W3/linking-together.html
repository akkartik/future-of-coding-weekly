The above link reminded me a lot of the Interface In Game gallery, which I believe has not been linked here before. Together, these two galleries are a fantastic resource for exploring various GUI moods.

HOWEVER ‚Äî there's a big gap here, and it's especially striking for this Interface In Game link. You can't actually touch and use these interfaces yourself. So you don't get any sense of how they feel (and in many cases, how they animate, how they sound, etc).

On the other hand, that constraint might be a good thing. Rather than being confronted with something so visceral that you can't help but take it as a complete, singular thing, by only getting a visual of the GUI you have just the taste of it in your mouth. You have to (and get to) fill in that big gap with your imagination.

Speaking of interfaces, this video of various Mac team members introducing MacPaint and MacWrite (with rapturous audience reactions) is priceless. Especially delightful is the Q&A, which starts around halfway through the video. Through the video, you get a good sense of a few things:

  • What particular aspects of these apps were surprising / delightful to a very tech-savvy audience.
  • What particular aspects of these apps are seen as "the thing" by various Mac team members
  • What sorts of technical and design tradeoffs the team made (becomes especially apparent in the Q&A)
