Chris Granger 2020-11-06 22:04:52

@Szymon Kaliski I don‚Äôt know that it‚Äôs insurmountable, but I suspect the solution won‚Äôt be obvious. Part of the problem is that counterintuitively navigation has become much more heavy weight. Instead of only dealing with one dimension of movement, you now have two and while you could just scroll in the current window, you‚Äôre then messing up your nice flow graph layout. What we saw in our studies was that people ended up generating lots of little boxes that they then spent effort trying to keep track of and garden. This is one of those things that doesn‚Äôt come up in most demo-y contexts, because the flow of navigation tends to be very unidirectional in the examples we give. But moving between those editors is a full blown context switch, whereas scrolling around is cognitively lower burden. This is why LT tried out some other ideas (three stacks where only the middle one is controlled by you and a stack itself acted like a document, documents made of disconnected blocks, etc), but they also had interesting issues, namely that it turns out files provide a kind of hidden context that‚Äôs important for both understanding and navigation.
