Present company is part of a https://english.stackexchange.com/questions/324569/does-the-phrase-present-company-excepted-exclude-the-speaker-as-well-as-the-li. Here's an example of how it's usually used: Let's say this is some high fantasy setting, and we're in a party together. I'm an elf, and you're a human. You might say outwardly "I've never met an elf I could respect," then looking at me, "present company excepted."

It's "company" in the sense of the people around you â€” both in the future of speech and as used for this channel. The word "present", then, has a double meaning for us â€” it's meant to mean both "here" as in we are all in this place, and "now" as in this is the channel where we talk about matters of the present day.

This is the channel where we all discuss matters of the here and now, drawing on the expertise and breadth of experience that this community has brought together.

Our other name ideas for this channel were all too narrow in their scope â€“ eg: #present-of-programming didn't leave the door open to discussions of slightly more off-topic things, which is something I want this channel to be available for. I also wanted to put the focus on the fact that the people in this community are all alive in the present and there's a value to us all being together.
